# How to Update Your Website with Cowork

This guide walks you through how to make changes to your website, preview them, and publish them live — all through Cowork.

---

## The Basic Workflow

Every update follows three simple steps:

1. **Tell Cowork what you want to change**
2. **Preview the change in your browser**
3. **Push it live to GitHub**

---

## Step 1 — Ask Cowork to Make a Change

Just describe what you want in plain language. You don't need to know any code. Examples:

- *"Can you update the tagline in the hero section to say XYZ?"*
- *"Change the color of the button to match my brand green."*
- *"Add a new section below the services with a short bio."*
- *"Remove the testimonials section."*

Cowork will edit the `index.html` file and copy the updated version to your **Dayna Website** folder automatically.

---

## Step 2 — Preview Your Changes

Once Cowork says it's done, open (or refresh) the preview file on your computer:

1. Navigate to your **Dayna Website** folder on your computer
2. Open `index.html` in your browser (double-click it, or drag it into Chrome/Safari)
3. Review the change — if something looks off, just tell Cowork and it will fix it

> **Tip:** Keep the file open in your browser while you work. Each time Cowork updates it, just hit **Refresh** (Cmd+R on Mac, Ctrl+R on Windows) to see the latest version.

---

## Step 3 — Push Changes to GitHub (Publish Live)

When you're happy with how everything looks, tell Cowork:

> *"Push this to GitHub"* or *"I'm happy with this — go ahead and publish it."*

Cowork will commit the changes and push them to the GitHub repository. Depending on how the site is deployed, it may go live automatically or within a few minutes.

---

## Tips

- **You can make multiple changes before pushing.** No need to publish after every small tweak — make all your edits first, preview them, then push once when you're satisfied.
- **If you want to undo something**, just tell Cowork what to revert and it will fix it — or it can roll back to a previous version from GitHub.
- **Cowork remembers the context** of your website within a session, so you can refer to things naturally (e.g. *"make that button bigger"* after already discussing a button).

---

## Quick Reference

| What you want | What to say |
|---|---|
| Make a content change | Just describe it in plain English |
| See the change | Refresh `index.html` in your browser |
| Publish to the live site | *"Push this to GitHub"* |
| Undo a change | *"Revert that last change"* |
| Start fresh from GitHub | *"Pull the latest version from GitHub"* |
